# World Rules

Source: migrated from `chris-lej/school-saga-1` commit `1f360388716a1ec9ca938be52f33ffcb3d793d05`

1. The world exists without the player.
2. The game never tells the player what to want; internal thoughts are observations, not instructions.
3. Curiosity must create meaningful possibility.
4. Attention is rewarded more than efficiency.
5. Relationships are felt, not measured.
6. Shared experiences matter more than dialogue grinding.
7. Rumors may be true, false, exaggerated, incomplete, or conditional.
8. Patterns are learnable through recurring routines.
9. Consequences create stories rather than simple good-or-bad outcomes.
10. Brazilian specificity shapes the world; it is not decorative flavor.
11. Ordinary life can carry wonder.
12. Humor should reveal people and create connection.
13. Emotion may be visually exaggerated at designed moments.
14. Lighting tells the emotional story before dialogue.
15. Achievements are rare acknowledgements, not a checklist.
16. Every feature must name the memory or feeling it is trying to create.
17. Generative systems may enrich expression but cannot replace authored coherence.
18. Ambiguity is welcome when it feels human rather than confusing.
