## Character principles

- Major characters should be inspired by specific memories rather than stereotypes.
- Fictionalization protects privacy and gives narrative freedom.
- Every character should have a life beyond reacting to the player.
- Reliability, humor, routine, relationships, and contradictions matter more than exposition.
- Characters may be core, supporting, ambient, or moment characters.

## Importance levels

### Core
Recurring characters with sustained arcs, multiple relationships, and meaningful influence on the player's year.

### Supporting
Characters with distinct routines and several memorable interactions, but limited long-term scope.

### Ambient
Recognizable people who make the school or neighborhood feel alive through repeated behavior.

### Moment
A person primarily attached to one memory, event, joke, rumor, or emotional beat.

## Character entry template

### Name
Working name; fictional by default.

### Inspiration source
Private reference to the real memory or person. Do not expose private identifying detail in public-facing material.

### Importance
Core / Supporting / Ambient / Moment.

### First impression
What the player notices before speaking to them.

### Routine
Where they tend to be and when.

### Social connections
Friends, rivals, family, classmates, teachers, teams, and changing relationships.

### Reliability
Truthful, cautious, mistaken, exaggerating, gossiping, joking, manipulative, or context-dependent.

### Shared-experience hooks
Activities or situations through which a relationship may naturally develop.

### Contradiction
A trait that prevents the character from becoming a stereotype.

### Possible memories
Scenes or moments the player may remember afterward.

## Initial roster placeholders

- Protagonist: player-created, fifteen years old, new student, no selectable background.
- Parent or guardian: present in the opening breakfast scene.
- First friend: unresolved; should be based on a specific memory.
- Classmates: target class size of approximately 20–25, with 8–12 eventual core or supporting personalities.
- First teacher: unresolved.
- Rumor source: one early character whose reliability can be learned rather than displayed.
